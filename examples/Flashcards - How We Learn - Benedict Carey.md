What are the two types of memory strength?::Storage and retrieval
What is memory storage strength?::Expertise on a topic
What is memory retrieval strength?::How easily you can recall a topic
What is Desirable Difficulty?::Memory strength increase, for a topic, is proportional to how difficult it was for you to recall
What is the primary benefit of the spaced learning compared to cramming?::Improved long-term information retention
What is the spacing effect?::Where studying for multiple shorter periods is better than studying for one long cram session.
How does testing take advantage of Desirable Difficulty to improve memory strength?::Forces you to recall information, which improves memory strength

==Blocked== practice is the opposite of ==interleaved== practice

Why is blocked practice perceived to be better than interleaved practice?::You appear to get better quickly during practice.
Why is blocked practice worse than interleaved practice?::You plateau more quickly and perform worse during novel testing
Define incubation (learning)::Letting your subconsciousness work on a tough problem in the background
In order for incubation (learning) to be effective, what stage of working on a problem do you have to be?::Stuck on the problem
Explain how sleeping on a problem is an example of incubation (learning)::You stop working on the problem, letting your subconsciousness passively think on it.
Define "fixedness" in learning::The idea that people make assumptions (unknowingly) about things that prevent them from thinking about it in a different ways.
Explain how the candle problem demonstrated fixedness::Tacks in boxes, people only saw a container of tacks. Tacks outside of boxes, people saw tacks and a container.
What are two easy ways to vary the context of your studying?::Changing when and where you study
